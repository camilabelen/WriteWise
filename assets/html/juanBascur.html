Juan Pablo Bascur, B.Sc., M.Sc.

Machine Learning and Scientometrics Researcher

Juan Pablo is a Biochemist and holds a Master of Science in Biochemistry (Universidad Andres Bello, Chile). As part of his thesis work, Juan Pablo studied the mutation of protein superfamilies in cancer, under the supervision of Daniel E. Almonacid, Vice President of Bioinformatics at uBiome. Notably, Juan Pablo developed new binominal distribution methods to search for genes involved in cancer, gaining knowledge in programming (Python) and computational techniques applied to solving biological problems. Juan Pablo has presented in five conferences, has two submitted papers, and has participated in government-funded research projects (Fondecyt).

Juan Pablo is currently a Machine Learning and Scientometrics Researcher at WriteWise. The computational insights gained during his thesis are now being applied to analyze scientific articles. More specifically, Juan Pablo is conducting advanced-level programing in Python and is managing a vast array of NLP and Machine Learning techniques. Additionally, Juan Pablo is developing new algorithms for representing and classifying text and for extracting features from scientific articles. All of the gathered information is undergoing further investigation using scientometric approaches towards journal recommendations and selection.
