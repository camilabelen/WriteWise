OUR QUESTION

Established in mid-2017 our group address fundamental questions on scientific writing using cutting-edge technology.

How we can help scientist to write and communicate better their research? What is the difference between a properly and poorly written paper?

What makes an easy-to-read and logic-flow written paper? What is the linguistic behind this?

How we can help editorials to improve and speed up review process through automatization?

How we make scientific writing review less bias and more objective? Is it possible to quantify a paper based on its writing?

OUR R+D

We are starting to answer these questions through state-of- the art Machine Learning techniques combined with Linguistics and Scientific Writing research, for developing new models, functions, and algorithms.

Through applied research our main purpose is to help and support researchers during the whole writing process. Our group is keen to improve scientific writing through developing new technology and writing guidelines. Our R+D+i is translated in a software called “WriteWise” which will not just modernize scientific writing, but also decrease time and effort for researcher when they write, but also for journals/editorial when they review.

Machine Learning
for Natural Language Processing applied to Scientific Writing

We use Machine Learning and Computational Linguistics that work together in the area of Natural Language Processing applied to the modelling and correction of Scientific Writing. Our work spans from:

1. Novel approaches for textual data representation for scientific articles:

Word embeddings to Deep/Machine Learning models for Natural Language Processing tasks.

Graph-based representations with a focus on using graphs whose vertices represent words and whose edges represent co-occurrence relations between pairs of words. This approach selects cohesive subsets of words and has several applications on text structure.

2. Novel computational approaches for scientific articles analysis. We specialized our research on:

  • Discourse Segmentation
  • Automatic Punctuation Analysis
  • Text Rule Mining
  • Topic Modelling
  • Readability/Coherence Classification

Rhetoric-Discourse
and Lexical-Grammar
Applied to Scientific Articles

We use functional and applied discursive frameworks combined with Corpus analysis, Computational Linguistics, and Natural Language Processing approaches for successfully accomplish the discursive and linguistics requirements of the academic and scientific genres through empirical-based research. Our work spans from identification and comprehension of the:

Communicative purposes and the lexical-grammar features that constitute a written genre in a scientific discipline.

Textual and discursive analysis of the academic and scientific genres
